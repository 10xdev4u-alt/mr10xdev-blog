3 daily habits of agent maintainers: review traces, talk to
users, ship often. The discipline that makes the difference.
The framework, the examples, the lesson.

## Habit 1: Review traces

The maintainer reads the traces. The maintainer sees what
the agent did. The maintainer learns.

**Daily practice:**
- Pick 5 random runs from yesterday
- Read the trace
- Note any anomaly, surprise, or pattern
- Share findings in #agent-traces

**Why:** the trace is the truth. The trace is what the
agent did. The trace is the way the maintainer learns.

**The math:**
- 5 traces / day = 25 traces / week
- 25 traces = 1 new insight / week
- 52 weeks = 52 insights / year

**The cost:** 15 minutes / day. Cheap. The lesson: invest
the 15 minutes.

## Habit 2: Talk to users

The maintainer talks to the user. The maintainer learns
what the user wants. The maintainer learns what the agent
missed.

**Daily practice:**
- Pick 3 issues from yesterday
- Reply to each issue
- Ask: "Was this helpful? What could be better?"
- Note the feedback in the issue

**Why:** the user is the teacher. The user knows the use
case. The user knows what's missing.

**The math:**
- 3 replies / day = 15 replies / week
- 15 replies = 1 new feature / month
- 12 months = 12 new features / year

**The cost:** 30 minutes / day. Cheap. The lesson: invest
the 30 minutes.

## Habit 3: Ship often

The maintainer ships every day. The maintainer ships a
small change. The maintainer ships often.

**Daily practice:**
- Pick 1 small improvement
- Implement it (10 lines max)
- Open a PR
- Get it reviewed and merged

**Why:** shipping is the rhythm. The rhythm is the
discipline. The discipline is the progress.

**The math:**
- 1 PR / day = 5 PRs / week
- 5 PRs = 1 feature / month
- 12 months = 12 features / year

**The cost:** 1 hour / day. The lesson: invest the 1 hour.

## The 3 habits together

The 3 are the discipline. The discipline is the progress.
The progress is the value.

| Habit | Time | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Review traces | 15 min | 1 insight / week |
| Talk to users | 30 min | 1 feature / month |
| Ship often | 1 hour | 1 PR / day |

The maintainer that has all 3 is the productive
maintainer. The maintainer that has 1 is the unproductive
maintainer.

## The 80/20

80% of the value comes from:
- Ship often (the maintainer makes progress)
- Talk to users (the maintainer learns)

20% comes from:
- Review traces (the maintainer debugs)

Focus on the 80% first. Add the 20% as you grow.

## The total

Total time: 1 hour 45 minutes / day. The maintainer
invests 1 hour 45 minutes. The maintainer gets 1 insight,
1 feature, 1 PR. The maintainer compounds.

The maintainer that compounds for 1 year is the
maintainer that has 52 insights, 12 features, 250 PRs.
The maintainer that doesn't compound is the maintainer
that has 0.

## The lesson

3 habits. 1 maintainer. 1 lesson: invest the time.

The maintainer that invests the time is the productive
maintainer. The maintainer that doesn't is the
unproductive maintainer. The productive maintainer
compounds. The unproductive maintainer doesn't.

The agent era is here. The habits are the design. The
design is the discipline. The discipline is the success.
