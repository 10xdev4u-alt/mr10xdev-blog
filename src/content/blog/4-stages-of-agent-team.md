An agent team goes through 4 stages: formation, storming,
norming, performing. Each has different dynamics, different
bottlenecks, different needs. The leadership lessons from 3 years
of running agent teams.

## Stage 1: Formation (week 1-2)

The team is forming. The agents don't know each other. The
agents don't know the workflow. The agents are learning.

**Dynamics:**
- The agents are tentative. They don't trust each other.
- The agents make mistakes. The mistakes are frequent.
- The agents are slow. The latency is high.

**Bottlenecks:**
- The user is the bottleneck. The user is teaching the
  agents. The user is reviewing every action.

**Needs:**
- Clear roles. Each agent knows its job.
- Clear boundaries. Each agent knows its limits.
- Clear feedback. The user tells the agent what to do
  differently.

**Leadership:**
- Set the vision. Tell the team what they're building.
- Define the roles. Tell each agent its job.
- Provide feedback. Tell the team what's working.

## Stage 2: Storming (week 3-6)

The team is storming. The agents are testing boundaries. The
agents are disagreeing. The agents are making mistakes.

**Dynamics:**
- The agents are confident. They think they know best.
- The agents are pushing limits. They want to do more.
- The agents are making mistakes. The mistakes are visible.

**Bottlenecks:**
- The conflict is the bottleneck. The agents are
  disagreeing. The user is the mediator.

**Needs:**
- Conflict resolution. The user resolves the disagreements.
- Clear boundaries. The user reminds the agents of their
  limits.
- Clear feedback. The user tells the agents what to do
  differently.

**Leadership:**
- Resolve the conflicts. Don't let them fester.
- Reinforce the boundaries. The agents are pushing.
- Provide feedback. The agents are learning.

## Stage 3: Norming (week 7-12)

The team is norming. The agents are settling in. The agents
are agreeing. The agents are improving.

**Dynamics:**
- The agents are confident. They know their job.
- The agents are reliable. The mistakes are fewer.
- The agents are fast. The latency is lower.

**Bottlenecks:**
- The process is the bottleneck. The process is too slow.

**Needs:**
- Process optimization. The user makes the process
  faster.
- Tooling. The user provides better tools.
- Documentation. The user documents the workflow.

**Leadership:**
- Optimize the process. The bottleneck is the process.
- Provide tools. The agents need better tools.
- Document. The team needs the docs.

## Stage 4: Performing (week 13+)

The team is performing. The agents are autonomous. The agents
are improving themselves. The agents are the team.

**Dynamics:**
- The agents are autonomous. They don't need the user for
  most decisions.
- The agents are improving. They update their own
  workflows.
- The agents are the team. The user is a member of the
  team, not the manager.

**Bottlenecks:**
- The vision is the bottleneck. The user sets the vision.
  The team executes.

**Needs:**
- Vision. The user sets the long-term direction.
- Review. The user reviews the team's output.
- Trust. The user trusts the team to do the work.

**Leadership:**
- Set the vision. The team needs to know where it's going.
- Review. The team needs feedback on its output.
- Trust. The team needs autonomy to do the work.

## The 4 stages together

The 4 stages are the lifecycle. Each stage has its own
dynamics, bottlenecks, needs.

| Stage | Dynamics | Bottleneck | Need |
|---|---|---|---|
| Formation | Tentative | The user | Clear roles |
| Storming | Confident | The conflict | Conflict resolution |
| Norming | Reliable | The process | Process optimization |
| Performing | Autonomous | The vision | Vision setting |

The team that moves through the 4 stages is the team that
succeeds. The team that gets stuck in one is the team that
plateaus.

## The 80/20

80% of the value comes from:
- Stage 1 (formation): clear roles and boundaries
- Stage 4 (performing): trust and vision

20% comes from:
- Stage 2 (storming): conflict resolution
- Stage 3 (norming): process optimization

Focus on the 80% first. Add the 20% as you grow.

## The test

The team is healthy if:
- Stage 1: each agent knows its job
- Stage 2: conflicts are resolved quickly
- Stage 3: the process is fast and reliable
- Stage 4: the team is autonomous

If any of these is missing, the team is unhealthy. Fix the
team.

## The meta-lesson

The 4 stages are the lifecycle. The lifecycle is the journey.
The journey is the value.

The agent era is here. The teams are the future. The
stages are the discipline. The discipline is the success.
